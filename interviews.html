Vidisha Bhambri

https://www.linkedin.com/in/vidbhamb/

Product Manager at Tatari

Interviewed: December 5, 2025


Selection Rationale

I selected Vidisha because she has extensive experience in both product and project management roles across high-growth companies such as Meta, GotTheTest, and Johnson & Johnson. Her background combines technical product development, stakeholder communication, and cross-functional team leadership‚Äîskills that directly connect to project management principles we are learning in class. Additionally, her MBA from USC Marshall and her transition through multiple industries make her a valuable stakeholder who can provide real-world insights into managing complex projects and aligning teams around clear objectives.

Conversation Summary

During the interview, Vidisha described her role as a Product Manager at Tatari and explained how product management heavily overlaps with project management. She emphasized that a major part of her role involves organizing timelines, defining requirements, coordinating with engineers and designers, and ensuring that product milestones are delivered on schedule. She shared that strong communication is the foundation of any successful project because misalignment can easily cause delays or rework.

She also discussed her previous experiences at Meta, where she led the development of a vendor portal, and at GotTheTest, where she built a healthcare product from the ground up. These projects required not only technical understanding but also structured coordination among stakeholders, risk anticipation, and continuous iteration based on user feedback. Vidisha explained that her responsibilities often include managing trade-offs between scope, time, and resources‚Äîcore principles in project management.

Throughout the conversation, she highlighted that adaptability is essential. Unexpected challenges‚Äîsuch as shifting priorities, technical blockers, or unmet assumptions‚Äîrequire the project manager or product manager to re-evaluate plans while keeping teams informed and motivated. She also emphasized that successful project leaders ask questions, clarify ambiguity early, and invest time in establishing shared context across teams.

Key insights

  • Clear requirements and aligned expectations reduce project risk. Misunderstandings at the beginning of a project often become the biggest sources of delays later.
  • Communication is the central skill. Whether managing engineers, designers, or executives, the ability to frame information clearly ensures smoother execution.
  • Adaptability is essential. Projects rarely go exactly as planned; the best managers adjust quickly and update stakeholders with transparency.
  • User needs should guide decisions. Even in internal projects, product and project managers must use feedback, data, and real constraints to prioritize features or tasks.
  • Career growth comes from continuous learning. Vidisha emphasized staying curious, seeking mentors, and taking on diverse challenges to build confidence and leadership capability.
